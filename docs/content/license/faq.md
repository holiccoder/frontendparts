# License FAQ

One license covers every paid plan — no per-component or per-site tiers. The short version: **build anything for yourself or your clients, keep the code forever, don't redistribute the library itself.**

## What am I allowed to build?

Unlimited **personal and commercial projects, including client work**. SaaS products, marketing sites, client deliverables, internal tools — if it's a website or app you (or your client) own, the license covers it, on any plan that includes the components you used.

## What can I not do?

Three things, all about protecting the library rather than your work:

- **No redistribution, resale or sublicensing** of the components themselves — you can't sell, share or give away the sources, in original or modified form.
- **No publishing a competing library** — don't repackage FrontendParts components (or close derivatives) as a component library, template marketplace, theme shop or similar product.
- **No public repos of the sources** — committing components to your app's private repo is fine; publishing them where others can lift the library is redistribution.

Your own app — markup, styles and content included — is yours to deploy, sell and hand over to clients without attribution.

## What happens when my plan lapses?

**Code you already downloaded is yours to keep using forever** — usage rights don't expire. What ends is access to *new* downloads, new drops and exports of paid components: a project that contains paid components asks you to renew before it will export again. Nothing in your shipped product stops working, and there is no telemetry or callback in downloaded code.

## Do I need to keep my subscription to keep using components?

No. A subscription buys ongoing access to the library; it never retro-licenses what you already built. Lifetime plans make this permanent — one payment, downloads forever.

## How many people can use one account?

The license is **single-seat** — one developer per account. A team tier with multiple seats is planned; until then each developer who downloads components needs their own plan.

## Which plan do I need for a given component?

Each component is marked **free** or **paid** in the catalog. Free components are downloadable without an account and usable under the same license. Paid components require any paid plan — Starter or Pro unlock the same 100% of the library; Pro adds scaffolding and export features on top (see [Scaffolding & GitHub Export](/docs/exports/scaffolding-and-github)). Trying to download a paid component without a plan returns an upgrade prompt, never a broken zip.

## Can I get a refund?

Yes — **14 days** from purchase, no questions asked. Request it from a support ticket in your [dashboard](/dashboard/tickets) (category *billing*) and the order is refunded through the original payment provider.

## Where is my license record?

Your [orders page](/dashboard/orders) lists every purchase with its receipt/invoice link, license state and renewal date — that page doubles as your proof of license.
