# Copyright & Takedown Policy

FrontendParts is built on a simple commitment: **recreate, never copy.** This page explains how components are sourced and attributed, and how to reach us if you believe something in the library infringes your rights — including exactly what happens after you do, and how fast.

## How components are sourced

- Components are **recreated layouts inspired by** live websites. Our authors study publicly visible pages and rebuild the layout from scratch as original React and Vue code.
- We **never copy** code, images, text, logos, or trademarks from the referenced sites. All sample content inside components is original placeholder text plus placeholder or free-license imagery.
- Layout ideas and general design patterns are not anyone's exclusive property; specific creative expression is. When in doubt, we recreate more loosely rather than less.

## Attribution

Every component carries a visible citation — **"layout reference: {site}"** — shown on the component page and in the preview modal header, linking to the site that inspired the layout. Attribution is a permanent part of the catalog, not an afterthought: if a component has no real-world reference, none is shown.

## Requesting a takedown

If you own rights in a referenced site and believe a component goes beyond inspiration, we want to hear from you — the channel below reaches the person who can actually remove content.

**The fastest path: open a [takedown ticket](/dashboard/tickets/new?category=takedown)** (category *Takedown* — a free account is all you need). If you cannot or do not wish to create an account, email the operator contact listed in the [Legal Notice](/legal-notice) with the same information.

Please include:

1. **Your work** — what site, page, or asset you believe is affected, with URLs.
2. **The component** — the catalog URL(s) of the component(s) in question.
3. **Your relationship** — that you own the rights or are authorized to act for the owner.
4. **What you want** — removal, stronger differentiation, or a change to the attribution.
5. **Contact details** so we can reply.

A short, good-faith statement that the use is not authorized is enough — no legalese required. Attachments (screenshots, comparisons) help but are optional.

## Our response SLA

- **Acknowledgement within 2 business days** of your ticket or email.
- **Substantive response within 5 business days** — removal of the component, an agreed modification, or a reasoned explanation of why we believe the component is a lawful recreation.
- Components under active review are **unpublished while we assess** credible requests, so nothing new is downloaded in the meantime.

Repeatedly unfounded or abusive requests may be declined with reasons. Valid requests are never met with legal threats — recreating more carefully is cheaper than arguing, and we treat rights holders the way we would want to be treated.

## Counter-notifications

If you are a contributor and believe content was removed by mistake, reply on the same ticket thread with your reasoning and we will review it under the same SLA.
