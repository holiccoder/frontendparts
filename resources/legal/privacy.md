# Privacy Policy

This policy explains what personal data the operator ("we", "us") collects when you use the Service, why we collect it, how long we keep it, and the rights you have — including under the EU/UK GDPR, the California CCPA as amended by the CPRA, and China's PIPL. The data controller is the operator identified in our [Legal Notice](/legal-notice).

## 1. Data we collect

- **Account data.** Name, email address, password hash, email-verification state, and your plan/entitlement state.
- **Billing data.** Purchases are processed by Paddle.com as **merchant of record**: Paddle collects and processes your payment method, billing address, and tax information. We receive and store only order metadata — plan, period, amount, currency, status, and Paddle receipt/invoice links — never your full card details.
- **Usage analytics.** First-party, server-side events about how the Service is used, tied — when you are logged in — to your account. We use no third-party analytics trackers for this.
- **Support data.** Support tickets you open, their threaded messages, and any attachments you upload.
- **GitHub tokens.** If you connect GitHub to export a project to a repository, we store an access token so the export can run. The token is used only for the exports you trigger, is stored encrypted at rest, and is deleted when you disconnect GitHub or delete your account.
- **Preferences.** Interface preferences (theme, preview layout, sidebar state) stored in cookies or your browser's local storage — see the [Cookie Policy](/cookie-policy).
- **Technical logs.** Standard server logs (IP address, user agent, timestamps) kept for security and debugging.

## 2. Why we use it (purposes and GDPR legal bases)

- **Operate the Service** — accounts, downloads, projects, exports (GDPR Art. 6(1)(b) contract).
- **Billing and license records** — orders, receipts, entitlement state (Art. 6(1)(b) contract; Art. 6(1)(c) legal obligation for tax/accounting records).
- **Product analytics** — understanding how features are used so we can improve the Service (Art. 6(1)(f) legitimate interest; first-party only).
- **Lifecycle and product emails** — onboarding and product-update sequences you can opt out of (Art. 6(1)(f) legitimate interest; consent where required); **transactional emails** (receipts, security, support replies) are mandatory (Art. 6(1)(b)).
- **Security and abuse prevention** — logs, rate limiting (Art. 6(1)(f) legitimate interest; Art. 6(1)(c) where legally required).

## 3. Who we share it with

- **Paddle.com** — merchant of record for payment processing, tax, and invoicing.
- **Email delivery provider** — to deliver transactional and opted-in lifecycle email.
- **GitHub** — only when you explicitly export a project, using the token you connected.
- **Hosting providers** — infrastructure that stores and serves the data under appropriate safeguards.

We do not sell your personal data, and we do not share it with third parties for their own marketing.

## 4. International transfers

Depending on where you are, your data may be processed in other countries where we or our providers operate. Where required (for example for EU/UK data or data transferred out of mainland China), we rely on appropriate safeguards such as standard contractual clauses. See the PIPL section below for China-specific terms.

## 5. Retention

Account data is kept while your account exists. Order and invoice records are kept for the period required by tax and accounting law. Support tickets are kept while your account exists and deleted with it. Server logs rotate on a short cycle. When you delete your account we delete or anonymize your personal data within a reasonable period, except records we must keep by law.

## 6. Your rights under GDPR (EU/UK)

You have the right to **access**, **rectify**, **erase**, **restrict**, and **port** your personal data, to **object** to processing based on legitimate interest, and to **withdraw consent** where processing is based on consent. You can exercise most of these from your settings (profile, notification preferences) or by opening a [support ticket](/dashboard/tickets/new). You also have the right to lodge a complaint with your local supervisory authority.

## 7. Your rights under CCPA/CPRA (California)

California residents have the right to **know** what personal information we collect, use, and disclose; to **delete** and **correct** it; to **opt out of the sale or sharing** of personal information; and to **non-discrimination** for exercising these rights.

**We do not sell your personal information and we do not share it for cross-context behavioral advertising** — so there is nothing to opt out of; if that ever changes, this page will carry a "Do Not Sell or Share My Personal Information" mechanism before any such activity begins. To exercise know/delete/correct rights, open a [support ticket](/dashboard/tickets/new) from your account (which verifies you) or contact us via the [Legal Notice](/legal-notice). You may use an authorized agent as the law provides.

## 8. Your rights under PIPL (China)

If you are in mainland China, the Personal Information Protection Law gives you the rights to **know, decide, restrict, and refuse** processing of your personal information, and to **access, copy, correct, and delete** it. Specifically:

- **Cross-border transfer.** Your account data is stored and processed outside mainland China. By creating an account you consent to this transfer; we apply the safeguards described in section 4.
- **Separate consent.** Where the law requires separate consent (for example for sensitive personal information), we will ask for it explicitly before that processing starts.
- **Account deletion.** You can delete your account from the settings area; deletion removes your personal information except records we must retain by law.

## 9. Cookies

Cookies and browser storage are described in the [Cookie Policy](/cookie-policy) — strictly necessary cookies only, plus optional interface preferences. There are no third-party advertising or analytics cookies.

## 10. Children

The Service is not directed at children under 16 (or the equivalent minimum age in your jurisdiction), and we do not knowingly collect their data.

## 11. Changes and contact

We will post any changes on this page with a new "last updated" date and, for material changes, notify account holders by email. Privacy questions and rights requests: open a [support ticket](/dashboard/tickets/new) or use the contact details in the [Legal Notice](/legal-notice).
